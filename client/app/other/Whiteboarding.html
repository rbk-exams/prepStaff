Interviewer Advice

The interviewer is very important--you are here to support your partner and help them improve.

Here are some tips to make sure this is fun!

Be Respectful

Whiteboarding can be stressful and scary for some people. Do not make it worse.

Don't Give The Answer

You want your partner to exercise his/her brain! It is no fun if you give the answer.

Finding the answer is not the most important part of whiteboarding.

Let me say it again.

Finding the answer is not the most important part of whiteboarding.

Okay?

The most important part is HOW the person thinks about the problem. What kind of questions do they ask? How do they think?

That is much more important than the answer.

Ask For Clarification Frequently

This means, ask your partner a lot of questions.

Make sure your partner explains what they are doing, even if you understand.

Ask them to explain what they are doing, each step.

Examples:

"Why are you writing a for loop?" "What are you thinking?" "What are some edge cases?"

Make sure your partner is talking! Don't let there be long silences.

Don't Be Controlling

Be careful not to do too much. You need to know when to allow your partner to continue. But if they are struggling too much...

Reduce Struggling

If your partner is having a hard time, please .

Ask them to explain what they have done so far. Give them a hint where they can look. If they mentioned an idea earlier, remind them about it. Ask them a question that you think will help them.

Leave Time for Review

Make sure you spend a few minutes after talking to your partner about how it went.
